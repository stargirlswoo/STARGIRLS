About

THE FOUNDERS

Sun and Moon, sisters and creative directors of STARGIRLS.

Sun and Moon, New York, 2026

STARGIRLS was founded by two sisters whose creative instincts developed side by side, but never in the same way. One moved first through emotion. The other moved first through observation. Their difference became the foundation of the work.

Sun and Moon began creating together before they had a name for what they were building. Music, image, performance, beauty and atmosphere were never separate interests. They were fragments of the same language.

SUN

Sun creates through instinct. Her work begins with feeling before explanation, with atmosphere before structure. She is drawn to memory, intimacy, performance and the emotional charge of a moment.

Her presence brings warmth, movement and immediacy to STARGIRLS. She is the impulse, the voice, the first spark of sensation.

“Beauty should be felt before it is understood.”

MOON

Moon creates through observation. Every detail is considered, questioned and placed with intention. For her, structure is not restriction; it is what allows a world to become permanent.

Her work gives STARGIRLS its architecture. She is the editor, the strategist, the eye that searches for coherence.

“Every object should have a reason to exist.”

DUALITY

The dialogue between Sun and Moon defines the identity of STARGIRLS. Their contrast is not softened or hidden. It is preserved.

Feeling and analysis. Instinct and construction. Performance and restraint. Together, these opposing forces create the visual and musical language of the project.

“We do not create from sameness. We create from contrast.”

BEGINNING

Their creative history began in childhood, through performance, rehearsal, recording and constant movement between cities. Music was present before it became a profession.

What began as habit became discipline. What began as expression became a shared direction.

IN THEIR WORDS

“Creation begins with intention.”
“Success is built.”
“Nothing replaces craftsmanship.”
“Beauty is everlasting.”
